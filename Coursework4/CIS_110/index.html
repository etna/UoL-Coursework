What is 'Academic Plagiarism'?
The best way to define academic plagiarism will be to split the term into is contituent words, define them seperately and then merge their meanings together. Oxford (“Plagiarism”, 2012, paras 1 – 4) describes plagiarism as an act in which:
  • any form of work belonging to another party is taken and passed off as one's own original content (“Plagiarism”, 2012, paras 1 – 4)

From this, we can infer that academic plagiarism refers to plagiarism that take place within formal institutions or organizations of higher education (“Academic”, 2012, paras 1 – 16).

What are the causes of Academic Plagiarism?
The most common causes of academic plagiarism are as follows:

  • Imperfect knowledge of required citation techniques
  • Stress induced by tight deadlines
  • Competition with peers
  • Pressure to obtain high grades
  • Indifference to intellectual property rights and copyright law
  • Laziness

Why are citations so important?
Citations can be thought of as the only 'tool' that academic institutions and plagiarism detection software recognize in acknowledging that the student has turned in an original piece of work that does not contain misappropriated content obtained from various sources. Failure to cite one's references correctly can result in the complete discarding of all citations by the plagiarism detection software, thus putting the student at an extremely high risk of having his or her assignment red-flagged by the software for plagiarism.

I cited my work; does this mean I'm safe from any plagiarism checks?
Not at all! Having citations in your assignment is only half of the equation: if you really want to be able to turn in your work without having to fear about the likelihoof of having it red-flagged by your academic institution's plagiarism detection software, you will need to ensure that your citations are done correctly. And by correctly, we mean that must conform to the specifications and requirements set forth by your institution.

Wrong / non-standard / incorrect citations do not matter as long as I have references and citations, right?
You are wrong! Most academic institutions have strict, no-nonsense policies regarding academic plagiarism, and ignorance is no longer accepted as an excuse to justify the act of plagiarism. In most cases, any incorrect citations will be disregarded by the examiners or plagiarism detection tool in use and the student will be deemed to have plagiarised any content that has been incorrectly cited.

What are the consequences of having wrong or non-standed citations?
The same consequences involving plagiarism apply to incorretly cited and referenced content. In most cases, once the plagiarism detection tool exceeds a certain preset threshold, the student will be deemed to have committed an act of plagiarism and may have his or her assignment scores invalidated, face diciplinary action or in drastic cases, may even face expulsion.
